Tumbang-Preso

Introduction

Tumbang Preso, also known as tumbang lata or bato lata, is a traditional Filipino children's game where players try to knock down a can or bottle with a slipper while another player guards it. The game is played in open areas like backyards, parks, or streets.

How to play Tumbang Preso?

One player or the tayà (the "It") guards the can. The objective is for the players to hit and knock down the can with the slipper, and for the tayà to put back the can inside a small circle a few meters away from the toe-line. When a player is tagged while recovering their slippers, they become the tayà.

History of Tumbang Preso

Tumbang Preso, a popular Filipino children's game, is believed to have emerged during the Spanish colonial period in the Philippines. The exact origins are not definitively documented, but it is thought to be a blend of Filipino traditional games and influences from the colonizers' culture and games.

The name "Tumbang Preso" translates to "knock down prisoner" in Filipino, which refers to the objective of the game: to knock down the "prisoner" (a can or bottle) with a slipper.
